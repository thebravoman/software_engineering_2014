< html > < body > Solid is one of the four fundamental states of matter ( the others being liquid, gas, and plasma). It is characterized by structural rigidity and resistance to changes of shape or volume. Unlike a liquid, a solid object does not flow to take on the shape of its container, nor does it expand to fill the entire volume available to it like a gas does. The atoms in a solid are tightly bound to each other, either in a regular geometric lattice( crystalline solids , which include metals and ordinary ice) or irregularly( an amorphous solid such as common window glass).
